About Me

Welcome, everyone, to my personal blog! My name is John Emmanuel Calanao, and I'm a first-year student pursuing a Bachelor of Arts degree in Psychology at the University of Southern Mindanao. I've created this blog as a dedicated space to showcase my journey through my Purposive Communication class. Here, you'll find a collection of my completed assignments, projects, and reflections, offering a comprehensive overview of my progress and learning experiences. I'll be sharing not only the practical aspects of the course but also my personal growth, highlighting the intellectual and emotional development I've undergone. Expect a mix of academic work and personal insights, providing a holistic view of my engagement with the subject.

This blog is more than just a repository for my assignments; it's a testament to my learning process. Each entry will delve into the challenges I faced, the strategies I employed, and the valuable lessons I've gleaned along the way. I aim to provide a transparent and honest account of my academic journey, sharing both my successes and setbacks. My hope is that this blog will not only serve as a record of my work but also inspire others to reflect on their own learning experiences and embrace the challenges of personal and academic growth. I invite you to join me on this journey and explore the world of effective communication through my eyes.

Activity 5: Social Issues

by John Emmanuel C. Calanao

This election season feels different, doesn't it? The amount of information—and misinformation—flying around is overwhelming. I've caught myself several times about to share something I saw online, only to pause and think, "Wait a minute...is this really true?" That's the struggle, isn't it? We want to be informed, but we also need to be discerning. So, I've been trying to be more mindful. I check multiple sources, I look at who's sharing the information, and I pay attention to the language used—is it designed to provoke an emotional response?

It's not always easy; sometimes, I still get caught up in the moment. But I'm learning to slow down, to question what I see, and to resist the urge to share unverified information. It's a constant process, a daily battle against the noise, but it's a battle worth fighting. Because our votes matter, and those votes should be based on truth, not on cleverly crafted lies. This election, I'm committed to doing my part to fight misinformation, one fact-check at a time.
